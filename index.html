A Modern Melville

Author of 14 novels, 2 memoirs, a short story collection, a children's book, and an Academy Award winning screenplay, John Irving tackles controversial and taboo themes with humor, aplomb, and keen insight into human nature.

Born in Exeter, New Hampshire in 1942, John Irving studied writing with Kurt Vonnegut at the University of Iowa Writers' Workshop. He published his first novel, Setting Free the Bears in 1968, and in the following decade, he taught at Mount Holyoke College and published two more novels.

His breakout success came in 1978, when he published The World According to Garp, which delves into sexuality, gender roles, and death. Along with Steve Tesich, Irving adapted the book into a screenplay. The film starred Robin Williams as the titular Garp, and co-starred Glen Close as his mother and John Lithgow as a transsexual woman who formerly played in the NFL. Close and Lithgow both received Oscar nominations for thier roles.

Irving continued to write, and several of his novels were adapted into films, including The Cider House Rules, starring Michael Caine, Tobey McGuire, and Charlize Theron. The film was nominated for several Academy Awards, including Best Picture, and Irving won for Best Adapted Screenplay.

His 14th novel, Avenue of Mysteries was published in 2015.
